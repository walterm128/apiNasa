Español:

Discovered on July 1 with the NASA-funded ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert, System) survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile, 3I/ATLAS is so designated as the third known interstellar object to pass through our Solar System It follows 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and the comet 2I/Borisov in 2019. Also known as C/2025 N1, 3I/ATLAS es claramente un cometa, su cometario difuso, una nube de gas y polvo que rodea un núcleo helado, se ve fácilmente en estas imágenes del gran telescopio de Géminis en Maunakea, Hawai‘i. El panel izquierdo rastrea el cometa mientras se mueve por el cielo contra estrellas de fondo fijas en exposiciones sucesivas. Se usaron tres filtros diferentes, que se muestran en rojo, verde y azul. En el panel correcto, las exposiciones múltiples están registradas y combinadas para formar una sola imagen del cometa. El origen interestelar del cometa también está claro a partir de su órbita, determinada como una órbita excéntrica y altamente hiperbólica que no retrocede alrededor del sol y devolverá 3i/atlas al espacio interestelar. No es una amenaza para el planeta Tierra, el intruso interestelar entrante ahora está dentro de la distancia orbital del sol del Júpiter, mientras que su enfoque más cercano al sol lo traerá justo dentro de la distancia orbital de Marte.

English:

Discovered on July 1 with the NASA-funded ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert, System) survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile, 3I/ATLAS is so designated as the third known interstellar object to pass through our Solar System It follows 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and the comet 2I/Borisov in 2019. Also known as C/2025 N1, 3I/ATLAS is clearly a comet, its diffuse cometary coma, a cloud of gas and dust surrounding an icy nucleus, is easily seen in these images from the large Gemini North telescope on Maunakea, Hawai‘i. The left panel tracks the comet as it moves across the sky against fixed background stars in successive exposures. Three different filters were used, shown in red, green, and blue. In the right panel the multiple exposures are registered and combined to form a single image of the comet. The comet's interstellar origin is also clear from its orbit, determined to be an eccentric, highly hyperbolic orbit that does not loop back around the Sun and will return 3I/ATLAS to interstellar space. Not a threat to planet Earth, the inbound interstellar interloper is now within the Jupiter's orbital distance of the Sun, while its closest approach to the Sun will bring it just within the orbital distance of Mars.
